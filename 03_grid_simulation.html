5 · Real-data layers
Critical facilities — real hospitals, fire stations, EMS, and water treatment plants from the federal HIFLD dataset. When a storm outage falls near one of these, it is flagged priority-1 for restoration instead of random 2% sampling.
NLCD tree canopy — per-substation canopy fraction from the USGS National Land Cover Database (30 m resolution, 2021 release). Replaces the simple urban/suburban/rural distance heuristic with actual measured tree cover around each substation.
Census tract population — demand points at census-tract resolution (~196 tracts) from the 2020 Decennial Census, replacing the 29-town centroid model for ~5× finer spatial granularity in outage customer-count estimation.
NOAA storm tracks — HURDAT2 best-track data for Sandy (2012), Isaias (2020), Irene (2011), and Henri (2021). Overlay the storm path on the map and weight outage placement by wind exposure along the track.
DOE OE-417 events — real major outage events from the Department of Energy Electric Disturbance database. Shows actual customer counts and restoration durations for calibrating the simulator against historical reality.
